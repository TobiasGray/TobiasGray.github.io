Phase 1

Creating this circuit enveloped a large portion of this unit. Here are the summarized steps we passed:

  1. Hammering 20 Nails in the holes provided by the circuit diagram
  2. Straightening these nails as they would provide the foundation for our circuit
  3. Using the multimeter to check whether or not these nails were straight
  4. Using the soldering iron to solder little blobs on each nail
  5. Bending and cutting wire, then re-melting solder and attaching appropriate wires
  6. Soldering resistors in the correct fashion, then soldering the capacitors to the wire similarly
  7. Using the same soldering method, soldering transistors, and finally the led bulbs
  8. Connecting the 9V battery connector to the circuit, and testing the circuit with a battery

For me, the most successful components to the circuit were the nails, soldering, and capacitors/transistors. The nails I hammered were mostly spot on, I rarely missed. I enjoyed soldering components because I had some small prior experience with soldering. I was able to solder effectively, with quite little mess. I struggled with the resistors as initially I had soldered them incorrectly. I realized I had soldered the incorrect resistor at certain spots, which led to unwanted delays and repetitive soldering.
